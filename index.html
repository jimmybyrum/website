  • For this last few years, I've been working with a startup based in Cambridge, MA. We make an app (Apple/Android) that lets consumers send a message to any business in North America and get a text response in minutes. I built the consumer website, a web application for our call center, and the Android app. I also do anything else that needs doing.

  • I took some time off in 2008 to be a volunteer English teacher in Chile. For six months, I taught at Liceo Polivalente Hernando de Magallanes in Porvenir, which is a small town on the Tierra del Fuego. After that, I traveled for six months, starting in Antarctica and then making my way north along the Andes.

  • I made two versions of the Yahoo! home page back in the day (2004) while working in Sunnyvale, CA. In 2006, I transferred to the London, UK office and worked on the European sites. I also worked on the Starbucks Digital Network project in late 2010 doing mostly mobile web development.

I have a couple small Javascript repos that are public and potentially useful.

  • Vault.js is a wrapper for localStorage, sessionStorage and document.cookie that sets and gets true javascript vars, objects, etc.
  • Crosshatch.js is a library to manage #! urls. Since creating this, I've switched to using Backbone which has this built in. That said, crosshatch is handy for very lightweight URL management.
